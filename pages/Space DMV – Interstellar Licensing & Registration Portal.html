Compliance & Safety Standards

Space DMV exists to prevent misuse of high‑energy, high‑velocity equipment that could become trajectory weapons. All registrations and licenses are bound by strict safety and accountability clauses.

Core Safety Principles

Every registered vessel and licensed operator is expected to uphold the following principles. These standards are designed to protect both the operator and any potential bystanders or unintended targets.

  • Trajectory Weapon Risk Mitigation: Vessels must be designed, operated, and routed to minimize the risk of becoming uncontrolled projectiles or weapons.
  • Operator Accountability Clause: The operator is responsible for all actions taken during vessel operation, including misuse, negligence, or situational mishaps.
  • Safety‑First Design: Equipment is built for safety, sharing, and collaboration, not for harm or weaponization.
  • Orbital Traffic & Exit Protocols: All atmospheric exit and orbital maneuvers must follow approved routes and timing windows.

Space DMV is not legally accountable for operator misuse of equipment that was designed for safety and collaboration. Accountability rests with the operator and any governing bodies overseeing their mission.

Interstellar Insurance & Legal Notices

Space DMV operates as a nonprofit regulatory division of Space LEAF Corp. It provides ceremonial and practical guidance, but does not replace local, national, or international law.

  • Interstellar insurance is strongly recommended for all registered vessels and licensed operators.
  • Off‑planet registration does not exempt operators from Earth‑based legal frameworks or international space treaties.
  • Space DMV records are maintained for transparency, stewardship, and future audit by cooperative agencies.

For full legal language, refer to the Space DMV License & Registration Policy document and the Space LEAF Corp nonprofit charter.
