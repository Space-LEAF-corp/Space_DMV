# Space DMV Charter 1.0  
*Foundational governance document for the Interstellar Licensing & Registration Authority (ILRA), a nonprofit division of Space LEAF Corp.*

---

## Preamble
The Space DMV exists to ensure that interstellar travel, atmospheric exit, orbital navigation, and hybrid Earth‑to‑Orbit operations remain safe, accountable, and aligned with the stewardship values of Space LEAF Corp.

This charter establishes the mission, authority, responsibilities, and ethical framework of the Space DMV.

---

## Article I — Mission
The mission of the Space DMV is to:

- Protect civilians, children, and planetary environments  
- Prevent trajectory weapon misuse  
- Certify responsible operators  
- Register vessels capable of leaving Earth’s atmosphere  
- Maintain transparent lineage of all off‑planet registrations  
- Uphold stewardship, dignity, and safety in all operations  

The Space DMV is **non‑governmental**, **nonprofit**, and **public‑safe**.

---

## Article II — Authority
The Space DMV is authorized to:

- Issue Space Operator Licenses  
- Register interstellar vessels  
- Assign Space VIN identifiers  
- Conduct trajectory safety reviews  
- Oversee off‑planet transfer lineage  
- Maintain orbital compliance records  
- Revoke licenses or registrations for violations  

Authority is granted through Space LEAF Corp’s Stewardship Council.

---

## Article III — Divisions
The Space DMV consists of three primary divisions:

### 1. Interstellar Licensing Division  
Responsible for operator certification, exams, renewals, and accountability enforcement.

### 2. Vessel Registration Division  
Responsible for Space VIN assignment, vessel classification, safety certification, and logbook audits.

### 3. Earth Subdivision Bridge  
Responsible for hybrid Earth‑to‑Orbit vehicles, atmospheric exit compliance, and off‑planet transfer lineage.

---

## Article IV — Stewardship Principles
All Space DMV operations must uphold:

- **Non‑weaponization**  
- **Child‑safe design**  
- **Civilian‑first ethics**  
- **Environmental protection**  
- **Transparency & accountability**  
- **Cooperative interstellar conduct**  

These principles override all other operational considerations.

---

## Article V — Compliance & Enforcement
The Space DMV may enforce compliance through:

- License suspension  
- Registration revocation  
- Mandatory safety reviews  
- Trajectory audits  
- Emergency beacon verification  
- Operator retraining  

All enforcement actions must be documented and publicly accessible.

---

## Article VI — Records & Lineage
The Space DMV maintains:

- Operator logs  
- Vessel logs  
- Space VIN lineage  
- Off‑planet transfer records  
- Orbital compliance reports  

Records must be preserved for future generations and audits.

---

## Article VII — Amendments
This charter may be amended by:

- The Space LEAF Corp Stewardship Council  
- The Interstellar Licensing Authority  
- A unanimous vote of the Orbital Safety Board  

Amendments must uphold stewardship and child‑safe ethics.

---

## Closing Statement
This charter ensures that exploration remains safe, collaborative, and worthy of the children who will inherit the stars.
